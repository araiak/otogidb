Data just came in for the next event so I'm sharing some initial impressions — and there's great news for Divina players, so let's get straight into it.

One caveat before we start: sources aren't confirmed yet, so treat every LJS / ranking / event / exchange call below as a prediction. The only ones we know for sure are the standard gacha cards, which are already active.

## Notable Picks

:card[1721] and :card[1720] are the headline for Divina players — the attribute finally gets a proper crit-rate carry, and these two are built to run together. :card[1721] is the carry: 40% Crit Rate and 40% Crit DMG at the start of the final wave, which makes him the best Divina carry option in shorter battles, and likely an LJS card. :card[1720] is the other half — 50% Crit DMG to the two highest-ATK allies, plus :card[739]'s massive team DMG-buff skill — and the two each gain 10 levels when paired, in kind of a :card[1526] / :card[1529] way. My guess is she lands as the overall ranking reward, but that's a prediction until the source is confirmed. In longer battles :card[553] is probably a little stronger if you have progressive crit from one of the many Divina sources (:card[1494], :card[1540] or :card[1672]), but not by a lot once the pair is rolling. :card[1720] is the more likely of the two to fall out of a team first, but :card[1721] can stand on his own when that happens, giving the cards a bit more individual longevity than :card[1526] and :card[1529].

Stacked against that :card[1526] / :card[1529] pair — the obvious template — the Anima combo is probably both more flexible and higher on the raw crit numbers: :card[1526]'s 40% crit rate / 50% crit DMG holds the whole battle instead of being gated to the final wave, and :card[1529] layers on another 20% Anima team crit rate plus 15% all-ally crit DMG every wave, with a skill that pushes team attack speed on top (it heals too, but the speed is the point), so it ramps from wave one and holds up in long content — where :card[1721]'s final-wave-only crit makes it best in short fights. What tips it back toward even is the company :card[1721] keeps. Divina has a higher final-wave crit-DMG ceiling (:card[1720]'s 50% on top of :card[1721]'s 40%), and the paired +10 levels matter more than they look: :card[1721] already out-bases :card[1526] on ATK (17,183 vs 16,706 at MLB), and because level-driven ATK multiplies outside your buff stack — and every additive DMG/crit-DMG buff in turn scales off that ATK — pushing his base even higher quietly lifts the value of the whole supporting cast. And that cast is the bigger factor: some unique Divina assist bonuses (:card[689], :card[723], :card[1443]), plus a real wealth of strong Divina team buffers (:card[1321], :card[1528], :card[1590], :card[1615], :card[1578]), means you can surround :card[1721] with team DMG and crit-DMG amplification the Anima pair simply can't match in depth. Both halves of the Divina pair also have objectively better skills — :card[1721]'s is a hard-hitting single-target nuke with a DMG-amp rider, and :card[1720]'s is the :card[739]-style team DMG buff — where the Anima pair leans on :card[1526]'s soft damage-and-slow and :card[1529]'s attack-speed buff (the heal is secondary), so the Divina cards squeeze more offense out of their skill slots too.  At the end of the day, which you build toward probably comes down to your preferred attribute more than any real gap between them — both are solid pairings, and these two are worth pushing for.

**:card[1721] — Must-have for Divina players — the best Divina crit-rate carry for shorter battles (likely LJS), and worth a mochi copy for most.**

**:card[1720] — Worth pushing for — likely a ranking card with 50% Crit DMG and a :card[739]-tier skill; strongest as half of the :card[1721] pairing.**

:card[1734] is a very strong assist, giving you 3 stat buffs: 40% skill damage, 40% crit damage, and 35% DMG to Phantasma (with silence, per Andromeda's ability). This probably isn't an assist you'd run on Andromeda — you'd want :card[1444] there — but on a secondary carry with a strong skill, this gives 35% DMG to all Phantasma, which could include your Andromeda carry with a :card[1444]. You could compare this card to something like :card[673], but with a little more DMG and more personal skill damage instead of team. So you'd want this on a card that hits fairly hard to get value out of it, but it's very strong. You'll have to do your own numbers, but for Phantasma this is a very strong assist. **Very strong Phantasma assist — 3 stat buffs (40% skill DMG / 40% crit DMG / 35% DMG to Phantasma); put it on a hard-hitting secondary carry, not Andromeda herself.**

## Standard Gacha

:card[1730] is currently active and is an ATK-heavy melee with a 2-target skill and self progressive ATK. Her stats are pretty high, but ultimately this card will struggle to find a niche. **Skip — high stats, but an ATK-heavy melee that struggles to find a niche.**

:card[1731] is very interesting as a wave clearer. She has progressive 15% DMG to Phantasma, which is larger than :card[740] and is great for endless Phantasma. That ability only unlocks at 4 copies, so a single copy isn't usable on its own. Since she's standard gacha I wouldn't pull — but if you don't have :card[740] or :card[744], grabbing one copy is still worth it: it gives you the option to limit break her the rest of the way with PvP orbs if you later decide you need her, rather than waiting to accumulate all 4 copies. Otherwise don't over-invest; just pick up copies over time from standard gacha, and once she's unlocked she should be very strong in endless and fine as a support and wave clearer in any multi-round content. **Don't pull (standard gacha). Her progressive 15% DMG to Phantasma ability only unlocks at 4 copies, so one copy isn't usable — but if you lack :card[740]/:card[744], grabbing a copy keeps the option to finish her with PvP orbs. Otherwise just accumulate copies over time. Beats :card[740] in endless or reserve.**

:card[1723] is also active. 55% skill damage on the final wave is an easy skip for anyone but new players who don't have a full set of assists. It's a fine single-buff stepping stone for a greedy attacker, but it's a dead end two ways: for a support slot you want an assist that gives one or more *team* buffs, and for a greedy attacker you'd rather save up for a multi-buff greedy assist like :card[1444] or :card[707] from auction, which gives you the value of several of these single-buff cards combined. **Easy skip — 55% final-wave skill damage; a single-buff stepping stone only for new players lacking assists. Build toward a multi-buff greedy assist (:card[1444]/:card[707]) instead.**

## Event Cards

:card[1719] is a somewhat defensive ranged support — she grants a small speed reduction to enemies and the skill throws around a slow to a random target. The random targeting is odd, and there are better defensive supports. Easy skip. **Easy skip — defensive ranged support with awkward random-target slows; better options exist.**

:card[1722] seems like it could be a half-ranking card. The second ability, which provides a 15% damage amp, is ok, but the 15% value is too low to do much. Passive damage amps are also a little awkward to play around, especially in content where damage amp is capped — these often lose value easily, or push value off other skills with damage amps. 5 copies is probably too expensive to see use for a card that is too weak when compared to stronger healers. **Skip — possible half-ranking healer, but a 15% passive damage amp is too low and awkward, and 5 copies is too steep.**

:card[1729] is a weird one — 2 copies for a 50% soul stone boost. This is probably not relevant for most players. If this becomes an exchange card I could see use for newer players, but even exchange price is probably a bit expensive for new players, where mochi is a very valuable resource towards building a team. Pretty easy skip unless it's very easy to obtain. **Easy skip unless very easy to obtain — 2 copies for a 50% soul stone boost; only situationally useful for newer players if it becomes an exchange card.**

:card[1735] is a relatively strong single-target buffer — 71% crit rate and 100% crit damage, on top of progressive skill damage. Single-target multi-buffs like this used to be excellent, but the niche is shrinking: now that all three attributes have their own crit-rate carries, that headline 71% increasingly *overcaps* on a carry that's already near the crit ceiling, so it unlocks far less damage than it would have a year ago — crit rate only pays off while you're still climbing toward the cap. She's still one of the best Phantasma single-target buffers, I'm just not sure it's a niche many players need right now. I'm going to guess this is an LJS card, and for invokers she's too close to the mochi card :card[655] to justify spending invokers. You could consider her if you don't have a crit-rate carry, or are willing to manipulate ATK values to get her onto the right target — if so, she should be a very strong Phantasma healer. If she's an event card she's a straight upgrade to :card[655], so that's worth grabbing for any player who doesn't have a solid Phantasma healer. **One of the best Phantasma single-target buffers (71% crit rate / 100% crit DMG + progressive skill DMG), but a shrinking niche — the crit rate overcaps once your carry already has crit buffs. Straight upgrade to :card[655] if it's an event card; for invokers it's too close to :card[655] to justify unless you lack a crit-rate carry.**

:card[1736] is a 3-target wave-clearer-ish card. She could have some play in endless as a greedy attacker, where both abilities would be very useful for clear times, but in most content skill damage and attack speed are both isolated buffs that don't synergize all that well, since they don't multiply off one another. I don't have any guess where this card comes from; if it's easy to get it's probably worth grabbing a copy, but it is a bit awkward to justify investment. **Situational — a 3-target wave clearer with potential as a greedy endless attacker; worth a copy if easy to get, but awkward to invest in (skill DMG + attack speed don't synergize).**

:card[1727] seems like a 4★ from LJS, but her DMG value here is nearly the same as :card[696], so new players may be able to save some mochi by comparing prices if they're still in the filling-out-assists phase of team building. This card and :card[696] are at best stepping stones — same story as :card[1723]: a single damage buff that's fine on a greedy attacker for now, but the moment you're past the early build you'd rather have a multi-buff greedy assist like :card[1444] or :card[707] from auction, and for a support slot you want something that buffs the team rather than one card. **Stepping stone — likely a 4★ LJS; DMG value ~= :card[696]. A single-buff filler; build toward a multi-buff greedy assist (:card[1444]/:card[707]) instead.**

:card[1728] is also potentially a stepping stone if it's easy to get your hands on, for a card that has a high crit rate. **Stepping stone — potentially worth it for a high crit rate if it's easy to get.**

:card[1732] provides a final-wave damage amp. This could be a stepping-stone support assist for players that lack assists, if it's easily accessible. **Stepping-stone support assist — a final-wave damage amp for players short on assists, if easily accessible.**

The rest of the assists are filler. :card[1726] gives 6% Crit Rate to all Divina, which sounds on-theme but mostly lands on cards that can't convert it — crit rate only matters on your highest attacker, who's already covered. :card[1713] is 13% damage reflection, a weaker take on :card[1453] from last month, and reflection stays about as useless in practice. :card[1717] (chance-based DMG amp on attack), :card[1718] (17% self Max HP), and the 3★ :card[1724] (3% team DR) round out the batch. **None of these are worth chasing — grab a copy only if it falls in your lap.**
