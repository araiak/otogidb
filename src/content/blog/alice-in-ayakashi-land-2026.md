Here's a first look at the new cards for Alice in Ayakashi Land. This is a quieter event compared to Elegance, which gave us :card[1526] and :card[1529] — both strong additions. Most of the cards here are situational or filler, with :card[1578] being the only one that looks like it could rise to that level. Acquisition details aren't fully confirmed yet — sections and recommendations will be updated as more information comes in.

---

## Event Cards

:card[1458] may be LJS. He has a greedy 15% crit damage and crit rate per round buff and a 3-target damage skill — nearly a copy of :card[1314]. I still don't see a great place for greedy progressive cards in most formats. They're fine in tower, story, and challenge, but if you can find a team buff that's usually stronger than a greedy AoE attacker. Where you really benefit is from a greedy single-target attacker, and diluting skill damage reduces your clear speed outside challenge. The ATK bond for ranged might make it worth grabbing a copy if he ends up on exchange and you don't already have double Phantasma ATK ranged bonds. **Worth a copy if he ends up on exchange and you need the ranged ATK bond; otherwise situational.**

:card[1576] is a 3-target progressive skill damage card like :card[1435] but with additional damage reduction. That might be ok in challenge mode but probably not your first choice. **Grab it if it's a tower card; not worth pushing for if it ends up as a ranking reward.**

:card[1577] is in the mold of :card[1489] but provides a [wild] melee slot for Anima. A lot of people are interested in seeing what they come up with for the card model here, but otherwise it isn't particularly special. :card[1539] is probably more reliably tanky with a worse skill in most modes. Of the [wild] cards, this is probably the strongest given it has damage reduction, attack speed, and HP restoration. It could also be a decent tank for newer Anima players if you can reliably trigger the ability — though ideally you'd want more support abilities from a tank. **If it's a tower card, grab an MLB; it's probably not worth pushing for otherwise.**

:card[1578] is a Phantasma Alice — unusual given Alice is Divina, whether by design or an error. Most players will be eyeing her for her abilities in world boss. Her first ability is a 2-target 45% damage boost similar to :card[672], which is great. Her second ability is a 10% time extension. Time extend is an interesting one — in effect it gives you roughly 10% more time to attack, which translates to a roughly 10% damage increase assuming your team is alive in a single round. There might be some edge cases around additional skill casts at the boundary. Where these really shine is with :card[514] due to her bug, which makes damage increase over time — in that case you get more than 10% because of the ramping damage. It could also have a similar effect in multi-round battles with progressive skills, and given that recent events have had shorter timers, the extra seconds may result in more reliable tower clears. Compared to other time extension options she is considerably stronger than :card[209] or :card[267]. Her skill is a single-target version of :card[739]'s skill, so she's usable in lineup but probably best in reserve. **Very worth getting and probably the top target for this event. Guessing LJS.**

:card[1579] looks like a potential ranking card overall. She gives 10% progressive crit damage to Divina and 25% attack speed. Divina has good access to attack speed and she may allow teams to more easily hit attack speed caps. Progressive crit damage is ok in multi-round content — 50% crit damage in 5 rounds is solid for a card that targets the whole team. Her skill is also notable: 71% crit damage lands on the cards that are going to do the most damage, especially in Divina where self crit rate cards are few and far between.

One thing to watch: she has some negative synergy between her skill and progressive crit damage. In multiple rounds she wants :card[1494] or :card[1540], but her skill will overcap with either of those in lineup or reserve. Her skill is very nice if you don't have those cards, or if there aren't enough rounds to ramp them, but if you do then the crit rate from her skill is probably too much and you might lose value vs swapping :card[1494] or :card[1540] into lineup and moving her to reserve instead. In 5 rounds you could argue :card[1578] or :card[514] are better reserves than her 50% crit damage. She is also a ranged ATK bond, which is a good reason to pick up at least a copy or two even if you don't see yourself using her in lineup. **Good and usable for a lot of rosters, but not top tier — worth getting if accessible, not worth breaking the bank for.**

:spoiler["Her attack speed is currently bugged — data suggests 10% not 25%. This would be a considerable loss making her much harder to slot into teams where she might justify a space today."]

:card[1581] also looks like a potential event LJS card. Her 30% critical damage looks decent but is probably beat out by :card[1992] — 30% damage is better in any situation where your damage isn't already extremely high. The damage buff on cast is too low to be strong. Her skill might have niche use as a cleanse, but so far there hasn't been much need for a cleanse in most content, though it's probably nice for some world bosses. **Not a priority — there are better options for the slot in most teams.**

:spoiler["She is currently bugged to only be 5% crit damage, making her a non-starter. It's probably safe to assume this will be fixed."]

:card[1582] looks like a 4★ or an exchange card — probably not a buy for anyone but collectors. **Pass unless you're collecting.**

## Standard Gacha

:card[1636] — cut-off art notwithstanding — is probably too situational to justify investment. It's in the standard gacha, so if you happen to end up with 4 copies it could be useful for adjusting critical rate from reserve. But 18% crit for Anima is probably not enough to bump other strong reserve options. If you're running :card[1526] and :card[1529], you'd get more crit by splitting off a copy of :card[1529] for the same reserve slot. If :card[1526] falls out of the meta then maybe :card[1636] could serve a similar role — crit rate is often very strong compared to other stats percent-for-percent, even when crit rate values are already fairly high. **Situational; only worth slotting if you happen to hit 4 copies and need Anima crit rate coverage.**

:card[1638] looks like a standard gacha option to revisit after the event. It has a little defense and a 25% shield debuff on all foes in the final round. This could stack with :card[642] or :card[661] for considerable shield debuff on teams that want more consistent damage or lack debuffers like :card[1311]. Values on these skills are a bit low and they require fairly high investment — the cost may be too high to justify for most players. **If you find yourself with copies it could be worth using if you need a mix of defense and shield debuff-based damage boost; not worth targeting.**

## TL;DR

Event Cards:
- :card[1578] — **Top target for this event**, probably LJS. 45% 2-target damage boost, 10% time extension; best in reserve, excellent world boss value especially with :card[514]
- :card[1579] — **Good, worth getting** but not worth breaking the bank. Progressive crit damage for Divina + 25% ATK speed; ranged ATK bond. Note negative synergy with :card[1494]/:card[1540] in lineup. *See bug note.*
- :card[1577] — **Grab MLB if tower card**. [Wild] Anima melee with damage reduction, attack speed, and HP restore; ok tank for newer Anima players
- :card[1576] — **Grab if tower card**; not worth pushing for if it's a ranking reward
- :card[1458] — **Worth a copy if exchange** and you need the ranged ATK bond; otherwise situational
- :card[1581] — **Pass for now** (bugged to 5% crit damage; fix likely incoming)
- :card[1582] — **Collectors only**

Standard Gacha:
- :card[1636] — **Situational**; only if you happen to pull 4 copies and need Anima crit rate coverage
- :card[1638] — **Niche**; shield debuff stacking with :card[642]/:card[661] if you have copies; not worth targeting
