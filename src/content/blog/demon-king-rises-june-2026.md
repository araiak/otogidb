This month we feature Demon King Rises, with a rerun of either Record of the Spirit Halloween or Maze of Red Leaves. The reason I list two is that there's some precedent for skipping seasonal events and moving them to special auction instead — so if you're holding out for :card[580], either this is your month or he could be back in October, or both.

---

## Event Cards

:card[1489] is the Conquest drop, and there's nothing special here. It could be a cheap MLB filler tank and that's about it — it's comparable to :card[1667] from the last event, and :card[738] from auction is the clearly better tank if you can grab her before supply gets low and prices go up. **Skip unless you need a cheap MLB filler tank; :card[738] is the better pickup for the role, with :card[1667] only comparable.**

:card[1498] is the half-ranking card, and she's a bit awkward. She could serve as a DMG amp skill with high crit rate for new players, but she suffers from being a melee card. Her survivability is fairly low because she's ATK heavy; the 50% crit rate is nice, but with a low base crit rate she ends at 57.5%, which is good, but you don't get crit damage with that base. The second ability, Five Great Swords, could be ok, but none of these cards are worth using on their own, so it costs an entire slot for 50% damage. She hits at least 2 checkboxes you want, so for a newer player without better options she's a real improvement — but look at her more as a stepping stone, and she'll probably be moderately expensive. With a carry you want the best card available, and she's just ok, so players who already have a :card[1401] or :card[1526] crit rate/damage combo can pass. **Stepping stone for newer Anima players without a better carry — you want the best carry available and she's just ok, so a :card[1401]/:card[1526] combo can skip; moderately expensive.**

:card[1494] is the overall ranking card, and she's a great pickup — but be clear about why. Most of her value is now in the XP niche: 20% EXP plus a 30% soulstone boost, which is rare and very strong in the one content type that rewards it, Special Story on Divina. In combat she's edged out — other Divina options give a bit more crit rate per round, and her XP does nothing for damage — so in Endless, Conquest, or anything that isn't an XP grind, :card[1540] or :card[1672] are stronger. But it's not by much: she's still a solid combat contributor, just not the top option there. None of that stops her being worth picking up: XP is hard to come by, and she's the strongest source short of chasing pricier LJS options. **Great pickup for the XP niche — a staple for Divina Special Story; value her for the rare XP, but she's still a solid combat contributor, only narrowly behind :card[1540]/:card[1672] there.**

None of the event 3★ and 4★ cards are of note.

## Limited Jewel Summons

:card[1453] is an interesting card. The 25% damage reduction in the last round can be useful, but there are better options in :card[661], :card[1590], or :card[1438]. This ability specifically can be very useful in World Boss, or in any format with a very high-damage boss that can derail things. In theory damage reflection is interesting because it goes around any offensive damage cap, but you'd have to be getting absolutely destroyed for it to do any real damage, so it's pretty useless in practice. I think this one is a skip — there are better options for last-round survivability and it's not worth spending jewels on. **Skip — better last-round survivability options exist (:card[661], :card[1590], :card[1438]); damage reflection is useless in practice.**

:card[2370] is interesting but pulls in two directions. The 75% skill damage is genuinely strong — it's less common than crit damage or flat damage increases, and it multiplies with them — but it only pays off on a high-damage carry that's already loaded with buffs. The defensive half, a 20% enemy ATK-speed slow, wants to sit on anything but a carry. So the two halves fight each other: lean on the slow and the skill damage lands on a support doing a fraction of your carry's damage, where 75% of not-much is still not-much; lean on the skill damage and you're handing a carry slot to defensive value instead. :card[1444] already owns the heavily-buffed carry slot, so there's not much room for her there. She's also about to be overshadowed by :card[1437] next month — 50% slow and 15% DR for all Phantasma is a strictly better defensive assist for the attribute. She's a bit more appealing on non-Phantasma teams, but the game has been pushing Phantasma hard lately. Where she could actually be fun is on a support caster like :card[690] or :card[725] — cards that function as defensive supports while still casting damaging skills. If you can spread buffs beyond your main carries, she'd hand one of these decent skill damage alongside the defensive slow. But that's pretty niche, and it's hard to imagine it nets much damage gain over other options. **Niche skip — the skill-damage and defensive halves want different homes, :card[1444] owns the carry slot, and :card[1437] is a better Phantasma defensive assist next month.**

## Rerun

The rerun is uncertain this month. The most likely candidate is Record of the Spirit Halloween, but there's precedent for seasonal events being moved to special auction instead, so Maze of Red Leaves is also possible. I'll cover both.

### Record of the Spirit Halloween

This is the most likely re-run, but it's possible it could get moved to special auction.

:card[579] is sufficiently powercrept. 20% damage to all allies is nice, but most players shouldn't spend jewels on a card that's only a minor boost to their team and will be replaced later. **Skip — minor 20% all-ally damage boost, powercrept.**

:card[580] is a staple drop rate and XP assist card — do I need to say more. He's required if you're trying to min/max XP/drop rate, but this isn't a required niche. It only takes 3 copies to unlock fully as well, which is nice in this day and age where cards most all require 5 copies. The only real question is the stock: when it came up the first time around, stock was very high, but many copies may be lost to time. If stock is very high you can try to buy, seal, and sell copies back — that's what I did on the first run myself. **Must buy for XP/drop-rate min-maxers (only 3 copies to fully unlock); otherwise fine to skip — watch the stock.**

### Maze of Red Leaves

Less likely but possible, so we'll cover it.

:card[596] gives 60% skill damage and a 20% chance to stun on normal attack. I imagine he could be very strong in real PvP, but very few people do that. Maybe there's some stun niche to attack-speed up and stun-lock something, but in most cases you're better off just blasting through. **Skip — PvP stun niche only.**

:card[597] is an attacker with a 45% slow at the start of each wave. 45% is actually quite high for a slow and does a good job reducing incoming normal-attack damage — but that's all she brings; she offers no team value beyond the defensiveness. Cards like :card[690] or :card[725] pair a slow with real team value (crit damage, damage amps, skill damage), which makes them the better picks for the slot — though both may be a while before they come around for a second re-run. **Skip — the 45% slow is high but purely defensive; :card[690] or :card[725] give both defense and team value, if you can wait for them to re-run.**
